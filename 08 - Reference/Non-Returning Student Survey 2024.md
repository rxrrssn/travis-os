# Non-Returning Student Survey 2024

Source: `08 - Reference/Non-Returning Student Survey 2024.pdf`

Author: Cassidy Krantz -- Data Science and Analytics (DSA), SLCC

Published: May 2024

Period: Students last enrolled Fall 2022 through Fall 2023, not graduated or re-enrolled by Spring 2024

Sample: 407 respondents out of 13,503 surveyed (low response rate -- ~3%)

---

## Purpose

To understand what contributed to students leaving SLCC before graduating or re-enrolling. Survey targeted factors the institution could reasonably act on.

## DSA Discussion (Key Points)

- Confusion with processes, resources, and requirements is something SLCC can act on. DSA explicitly calls this a "viable intervention for decreasing early exit."
- Financial constraints are the leading contributor but fall outside SLCC's immediate control.
- Improving information accessibility is identified as a primary actionable lever.

---

## Relevance to IVR Call Routing Analysis

This survey does not directly measure phone friction, but it provides institutional corroboration for the confusion-to-exit argument in the [[IVR Call Routing Analysis]] one-pager and the [[Bruin Connect Transition]] proposal.

**The connection:**

1. Confusion was the third most cited category (42% of non-returners). The #1 confusion factor was Accessing Advising/Guidance (98 responses). Students who cannot navigate to the right support are a documented attrition risk.

2. SLCC Website & Online Resources was cited by 66 respondents as a registration-related contributor to leaving. The website currently publishes direct department and personnel phone numbers -- students who call the wrong number and get bounced through IVR menus and transfers are experiencing a direct manifestation of this documented confusion.

3. DSA's own recommendation -- "making information more accessible would likely be viable interventions for decreasing early exit" -- maps directly to what reducing IVR friction achieves. This allows the one-pager to cite internal institutional research without overstating a causal link.

**Suggested citation framing:** "SLCC's 2024 Non-Returning Student Survey found that confusion around accessing advising and guidance was the most common form of student confusion, cited by 42% of non-returning students. DSA identified improving information accessibility as a viable intervention for reducing early exit. Simplifying how students reach support by phone is a direct and measurable implementation of that recommendation."
