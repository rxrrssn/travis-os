# OSC Executive Summary and Program Report -- Spring 2026

Source: `08 - Reference/OSC Executive Summary and Program Report Spring 2026.pdf`

Author: OSC team / Travis Hornbuckle

Period: Spring 2026 (data spans FA23-SU25 effort metrics; cost analysis from AY 2022-23)

## Executive Summary

### Why OSC Matters Now

- 18-22% of SLCC students enrolled exclusively online; 40%+ of course sections offered online in peak terms
- Online learning scale creates risk of early disengagement, stop-out, and unmet support needs
- OSC functions as both a student success intervention AND an enrollment stabilization strategy

### Key Impact Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Persistence rate (3+ coaching connections) | 98% |
| Persistence rate (fewer than 3 connections) | 61% |
| Likelihood multiplier (3+ vs non-engaged) | 60.5% more likely to persist |
| Coached students in good academic standing | 78% |
| Non-engaged students in good academic standing | 27% |
| GPA increase (3+ connections) | +0.15 average |
| Students reached annually | ~5,400 |
| Connection rate (assigned students) | 63% |
| Total annual program cost | $423,789.59 |
| Cost per student per year | $78.48 ($39.24/semester, $6.54/month) |

## Program History and Development

- Established Fall 2016 within eLearning department
- Originally 2 coaches + support specialist, cohorts of 125 students per coach
- 2016-2018: cohort sizes grew from 125 to 200 per coach
- 2019: reached 400 students per coach (current standard)
- Outreach start adjusted from 4 weeks to 2 weeks before semester (students registering later)
- Now accepting referrals and moving toward campus-wide access beyond assigned cohorts
- Metrics source: IBM Cognos Analytics (cohort generation via Institutional Research)

### Target Population

Students in assigned cohorts meet one or more criteria:
- Enrolled in at least two online courses
- Fewer than 15 completed credits
- Academically at-risk: probation, first-generation, new to college, or enrolled in condensed 8-week/half-term courses

## Program Tools

### Strong Start Campaign
Pre-semester outreach (begins 2 weeks before semester). Phone calls + personalized intro email including coach photo. Goal: establish relationship, understand student motivation and academic goals.

### OSC Canvas Course
Structured coaching course with weekly modules: orientation, academic skills, soft skills. Motivational Mondays auto-emailed each week via Canvas. Monthly check-in forms. Beginning SP26: students self-enroll. Canvas engagement peaks before semester start and during first 3 weeks.

### Outreach Tracker
Internal spreadsheet updated daily. Tracks weekly contact, connections, preferences, notes per student. Coaches use for at-a-glance trend identification and call prioritization.

### Grade Tracker (BA-1125)
Built with DSA (Department of Statistics and Analytics). Daily grade data from Canvas.
- Emerging Learners: score <= 60% AND no Canvas login for 5+ days
- Star Students: at least one course >= 90% AND no courses below 70% (recognized monthly)

### MySuccess (Starfish by Ellucian)
Case management, early alert flags, appointment scheduling, cross-departmental notes. OSC manages flag work for broader SLCC Care Network beyond just their own cohorts.

## Strategic Signals and Future Direction

- Starting SP26: monitoring third-week churn and first-week Canvas login as early risk indicators
- Frontline Enrollment Intelligence: coaching interactions surface stop-out signals before they appear in enrollment reports
- Moving toward campus-wide open coaching (not just assigned cohorts)
- Referral system active
- Survey participation is a known gap -- strategy needed to improve data quality
- Vision Matrix alignment: Engage (Access & Momentum) + Complete (Progression)

---

## Strategic Observations

- 98% vs 61% persistence split at the 3-touch threshold is the single most powerful metric OSC has -- every leadership conversation should lead with this
- $78.48/student/year is nearly impossible to beat as a cost argument; use it against the "cut programs" pressure from HB 265 fallout
- 63% connection rate in an opt-in program with a remote, at-risk population is genuinely strong
- The Star Student recognition program (BA-1125) and Emerging Learners report are infrastructure Travis is positioned to extend or integrate into broader TDX/Power BI workflows
- OSC's flag resolution rate (95.9%) makes them a de facto triage layer for the Care Network -- their role is broader than their title suggests
