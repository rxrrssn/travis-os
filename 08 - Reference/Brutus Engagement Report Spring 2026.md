# Brutus Engagement Report -- Spring 2026

Source: `08 - Reference/Brutus V2 Spring 2026.pdf`

Author: Travis Hornbuckle (extrapolated from raw data, produced for senior leadership presentation. V2 reflects Travis's own design iteration -- content and framing unchanged from V1.)

Attribution on document: Enrollment Management | Contact Center

Period: 2023 - Present (reported Spring 2026)

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Students Reached | 218,050 |
| Questions Received | 194,419 |
| Messages Exchanged | 2,850,000 |
| Resolved Automatically | 98.76% |
| Engagement Campaigns Run | 136 |

---

## Conversation Breakdown by Category

| Category | Share |
|---|---|
| Registrar | 27.1% |
| Admissions | 23.5% |
| Student Services | 18.1% |
| Advising | 16.9% |
| Campus Services | 10.3% |
| Bursar | 2.2% |
| Financial Aid | 2.0% |

**Category definitions:**
- Registrar: registration, transcripts, grades, degree requirements
- Admissions: applying, application status, placement testing, new student onboarding
- Financial Aid: FAFSA, aid eligibility, scholarships, loans, verification
- Bursar: tuition charges, payments, account balances
- Student Services: tutoring, counseling, disability support, student systems
- Campus Services: parking, locations, transportation, facilities

Note: Institutional Marketing campaign replies are not captured in the category breakdown but reach students across Enrollment Management.

---

## Strategic Observations

- 98.76% automatic resolution is an exceptionally strong containment rate -- most students get answers without staff intervention
- Registrar + Admissions alone account for over 50% of conversations, suggesting these are the highest-friction navigation areas
- Financial Aid at only 2% is likely underrepresented given the complexity of FA questions -- may reflect routing to live staff rather than bot resolution
- 136 campaigns indicates active outreach use, not just passive FAQ support
- 2.85M messages across 218k students reached = ~13 messages per student on average

---

## Relevance

This report directly supports the Bruin Connect transition narrative. Brutus is already functioning as an institutional triage and navigation layer at scale. Combined with the Contact Center's 85k calls and 2,500 hours of student time lost to IVR overhead, this data makes a strong case for a unified, centralized support model.
