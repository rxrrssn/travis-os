# SLCC Trajectory and the Unmasking

Synthesis from the 7/16 session. Builds on [[Travis SLCC Positioning]].

## The mask

The Contact Center label is a permission structure, not just camouflage. It makes institutional-design and strategy work permissible at the coordinator level — without it, a coordinator doing institutional strategy reads as overreach — and it defuses turf threat to the senior leaders whose lane strategy actually is. That is why the work gets done under a CC banner.

The same cover caps it. Work filed under "Contact Center operations" accrues to the CC function, not to me as an institutional strategist. The mask enables and ceilings at once. And it has a shelf life: worn long enough, "the helpful CC guy" hardens from costume into identity, and the eventual relabel gets harder.

## The unmasking

The real move is relabeling the work from operations to institutional design, in front of the people who draw the org chart — and it is safest sponsor-led, not self-led. Me naming myself an institutional strategist reads as grandiosity; [[Seini Pahulu]] or [[Ryan Farley]] naming the work that way reads as fact. That unmasking has already begun: Seini moving into Institutional Effectiveness, pulling me toward it, and handing me the SLCC 360 co-present slot (metrics laddering to institutional goals, in the [[HB 265]] data-driven moment). The destination is the one seat where mask and work are the same thing: IE / operational planning, where institutional design is the job description.
