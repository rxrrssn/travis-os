# WGU Degree Plan

The formal credential path: **WGU Accelerated BSIT → MSIT (Product Management)**, then **South College DBA (IT specialization)**. Two specific WGU programs drive the plan — the [Accelerated BS Information Technology](https://www.wgu.edu/online-it-degrees/information-technology-bachelors-program/accelerated.html) and the [MS IT Product Management](https://www.wgu.edu/online-it-degrees/information-technology-masters-program/product-management.html) — packaged as a combined BS→MS bridge of roughly **42–43 total courses**, with certain "Shared" courses counting toward both degrees. The strategy is competency-based speedrunning: formalize work already being done in enterprise ops, automation, BI, governance, and identity, rather than learning from zero.

## Enrollment & Financial Aid

- **Term start:** June 1, 2026. WGU runs fixed **6-month terms**, so the first BSIT term ends approximately **end of November 2026**.
- **Tuition:** ~**$4,152** balance per 6-month term (flat rate; WGU's published range ~$3,945–$4,090). Pay-per-term, unlimited CUs — the more courses cleared, the cheaper the effective cost per credential.
- **MPN snag:** MPN completed **April 30, 2026** and visible on StudentAid.gov, entrance counseling processed — but WGU had not ingested the MPN nearly **a month later**, an orphaned processing item on the school side, not an eligibility problem. Workaround: agreed to a **pay-in-full payment plan** purely to clear the onboarding/registration gate, with intent to cancel once aid posts. Autopay drafts on the 1st, so the risk window is aid posting before the draft.
- **Prior loans:** ~**$10k** in associate-degree loans, repayment starting **July 2026**; active enrollment triggers in-school deferment. WGU counts enrollment by **competency units per term** (minimum **8 CUs/term** to stay full-time; graduate side same rule).

## Transfer Credit

- **33 CUs / 11 courses** transferred in from the dual associates (**AS Psychology** + **AS Business Administration, Data Analytics concentration**) — this is the applied acceleration, roughly a quarter of the ~120-CU BSIT cleared before day one.
- **Sophia Learning** was evaluated as an additional pre-enrollment accelerant (ACE-recommended gen-ed/lower-division courses, ~75% transfer cap across all sources, program-specific mapping) but was **not** the source of the applied credit — the associate transfer covered it.

## First Term Structure & Pacing Reality

WGU frontloaded a controlled first term: a **mentor-committed block of 5 courses / 15 CUs** with **2-week minimum windows per course** and mentor approval required before registration unlocked. Mentor forecast ITIL at **3 weeks**. The committed Term 1 block:

| Code | Course | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| ORA4 | Orientation | Administrative |
| E004 | Introduction to IT | Survey-level |
| E005 | Business Productivity Software | 3-CU PA |
| D336 | Business of IT – Applications | **ITIL 4 cert** |

The pacing model is the friction point: WGU throttles first-term velocity for SAP/aid compliance and mentor oversight, then loosens as demonstrated throughput proves out. The real bottleneck became **administrative** (mentor course-unlock throughput, voucher/exam scheduling, evaluator turnaround) rather than academic — the profile of a one-term finisher who outruns the university's own processing.

## BSIT → MSIT Transition

- **Two separate accredited degrees, two diplomas, two conferrals** — not one combined degree. Each has its own graduation application.
- Transition handled as a **returning/continuing graduate** (streamlined), not a fresh application.
- **Hard constraint:** even if BSIT finishes in October, MSIT cannot start until the **next 6-month term** after the current one ends (~end of November 2026). WGU does not end a term early. Optimization: get continuing-graduate/MSIT paperwork queued **before** the BSIT term ends to avoid administrative dead time on the transition.
- **MSIT target completion:** ~**January–February/March 2027** if pace holds.
- **MSIT (Product Management) courses** align tightly with Corveaux thinking: Cloud Strategy, Cybersecurity Strategy, Data & AI Strategy, Leading a Technology Transformation. Shared/double-count courses (E015, E012, E011) reduce total MSIT load.
- **Open decision — MSIT vs MBA-ITM:** MSIT signals a technical/systems/product leader (cleaner fit for current identity and Corveaux architecture work); MBA-ITM signals a business executive/founder and may age better toward a CEO/operator endgame. Current enrollment is MSIT Product Management; the swap remains an open question.

## DBA Follow-On (South College)

Concrete doctoral plan is the **South College competency-based DBA, Information Technology specialization** (competency-based, strong 1:1 faculty interaction, niche program — the CBE community is only ~150–200 members). Coursework is largely shared across DBA specializations until the dissertation focus.

- **Start:** ~**February/March 2027** (directly after MSIT). Well into it by June 2027.
- **Dissertation sequence:** ~**11 courses**, each ~**15 assignments**, individually graded in **0–3 days** (~2-day average → ~30 days/course; heavier stretches ~45 days/course). First two courses can run **concurrent**.
  - At 45 days/course: 11×45 − 45 = **450 days** (~14.8 months)
  - At 30 days/course: 11×30 − 30 = **300 days** (~10 months)
- **Batching strategy:** submission is sequential (one assignment at a time), so the grader is the bottleneck. Stay **5–10 assignments ahead** within a course, pre-writing offline so each posted grade just triggers an instant next submission. Keeps writing off the critical path.
- **Estimated DBA completion:** ~**mid-2028** if turnaround stays favorable.
- Either a traditional dissertation or an applied capstone (both doctoral-level culminating projects) — the applied "build a validated governance/reference framework" route fits the build-while-learning style. Likely **research domain** (not a fixed topic yet): enterprise architecture, digital transformation, technology/information governance, AI governance — with Corveaux as an implementation example, not the research subject.

**All-in cost** for dual AS + BSIT + MSIT + DBA is projected at ~**$31,000** — well below the $78k–$230k+ typical U.S. sticker for that credential stack.

## Progress Tracker Design

Purpose-built Excel workbook treating the degree stack as a project, not a course list. Sheets: **Dashboard, Courses, Certifications, Lookups, DBA Planner**. Core mechanics:

- **Difficulty weighting** per course (an Easy 3-CU course and Network+ don't contribute equally to ETA).
- **Hours logged** — effort measured, not just completion, given nonlinear WGU progress.
- **Certification sheet** tied back to source courses.
- **ETA formula** — best run as a **rolling average of the last 3–5 completed courses** rather than lifetime average, since pace comes in bursts.
- Planned additions: milestone timeline, remaining-study-hours counter, velocity graph (hours/week, CUs/month), certification chain, GPA-equivalent metrics (avg completion days, hours/CU, first-attempt pass rate), Knowledge-Area column, and a DBA Research-Topic column.
- **Remaining study-hour estimates:** BSIT ~**312 hrs**, MSIT ~**181 hrs**. DBA dissertation budgeted at **150 hrs** — flagged as optimistic; realistic budget is **250–400 hrs** for an applied IT dissertation while working full time.
