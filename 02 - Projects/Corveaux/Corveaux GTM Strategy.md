# Corveaux GTM Strategy

## Core Messaging Principle

Do NOT position Corveaux as an integration platform. Higher ed is already drowning in middleware, connectors, ETL jobs, sync failures, shadow systems, and duplicate authority. Calling Corveaux "another integration layer" loses immediately.

The correct framing:

- **Internal truth:** Corveaux is the future system of record. The revolution happens behind the scenes.
- **External message:** Continuity. Simplification. Gradual modernization. Operational consolidation. Governance improvement.

The revolution cannot sound like a revolution to institutional IT.

---

## The Real Strategic Position

Banner, Canvas, Salesforce, Workday -- all domain-specific systems with fragmented authority requiring synchronization layers.

The thesis:

**The institution itself should be the platform boundary -- not the vendor product boundary.**

That is the core ideological distinction from every incumbent in the space.

---

## Transitional Coexistence Strategy

Corveaux cannot say "rip out Banner tomorrow." Institutional IT leaders are risk-averse because ERP migrations fail constantly, institutional memory is fragile, and downtime is career-ending.

The practical positioning: **"We can coexist with your current ecosystem while progressively reducing institutional complexity."**

### Phase 1 -- Observe

Corveaux ingests from: Banner, Canvas, Salesforce, Active Directory/Entra, etc.

Not to integrate forever. To:
- Model institutional reality
- Establish canonical relationships
- Reduce friction
- Deliver immediate operational value

Legacy systems remain authoritative. Corveaux watches and learns.

### Phase 2 -- Orchestrate

Corveaux begins owning:
- Workflows
- Communications
- Lifecycle automation
- Approvals
- Service orchestration
- Identity triggers

Legacy systems become transactional backends. Corveaux is now the operational layer.

### Phase 3 -- Absorb

Over time:
- Functionality migrates into Corveaux
- Dependency on legacy systems shrinks
- Duplicate systems disappear

Eventually Banner, Salesforce, and middleware become unnecessary -- not because Corveaux forced them out, but because they stopped being needed.

## Module Wedge Sequence

### 1. Web / CMS / Knowledge (Primary Wedge)

Best entry point:
- No regulatory risk (pre-FERPA territory)
- Visible institutional pain
- Cross-department exposure immediately
- Easier procurement path
- Builds canonical knowledge infrastructure everything else depends on

**Why this wedge is deeper than it appears:**

To render a website correctly, Corveaux needs: departments, people, courses, policies, locations, programs. That data has to come from somewhere -- Banner, AD, the course catalog, HR systems. So even a CMS sale begins ingesting from authoritative systems immediately, for a completely non-threatening reason (making the website work).

This is how Phase 1 (Observe) begins in practice. The institution gives Corveaux read access to existing systems to power web content. Corveaux starts modeling institutional reality. By the time the institution realizes Corveaux has a more coherent picture of their structure than their own systems do, the canonical layer is already being built.

The wedge is not just low-risk. It is the trojan horse for the canonical data model.

### 2. Communications / Campaigning / Chatbot

Natural second move. Once Corveaux owns content, knowledge blocks, and departmental structure, communications become operational:
- Lifecycle nudges
- Outreach campaigns
- AI assistants
- Segmentation

Directly aligned with Mainstay / Contact Center expertise and the One Voice philosophy.

### 3. Student Success / Advising

With communications and identity context established:
- Case management
- Success workflows
- Advisor assignment
- Intervention orchestration

### 4. Workflow / Service Management

Corveaux becomes operational infrastructure:
- Requests and approvals
- Onboarding workflows
- Internal service routing

The TDX philosophy, generalized institution-wide.

### 5. SIS / ERP Core

Only after trust, dependency, institutional embedding, and canonical data maturity are established.

SIS replacement is the hardest sell in higher ed. You do not lead with this. You absorb toward it.

---

## Political Reality

Institutional IT leaders fear:
- Failed ERP migrations
- Loss of institutional memory
- Integration complexity
- Downtime and accountability

Corveaux must externally sound like: gradual modernization, operational consolidation, risk reduction.

Internally it is: progressive system-of-record displacement.
