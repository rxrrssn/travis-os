# Corveaux Data Portability

## Philosophy

Higher ed is traumatized by vendor lock-in, opaque schemas, and impossible migrations. Corveaux should aggressively position itself as institution-owned data architecture. Trust is a strategic advantage.

## Likely Principles

- Full export access at any time
- Documented, open schemas
- Open APIs
- Event / webhook access
- Tenant-level backups
- Institution-owned knowledge and content

## Potential Features

- "Institution continuity guarantees"
- Escrow / export tooling
- Archival snapshots
- Data residency options

## Positioning

Institutions that are afraid to adopt Corveaux because of lock-in concerns are the exact institutions that need it most. Leading with portability commitments removes the barrier and differentiates from incumbent vendors who profit from opacity.
