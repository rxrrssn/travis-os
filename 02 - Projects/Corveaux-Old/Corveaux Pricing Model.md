# Corveaux Pricing Model

## Philosophy

Pricing should feel like: replacing 6 systems, not buying 1 more.

Community colleges cannot absorb Workday-style pricing, per-click AI fees, or massive implementation retainers. Corveaux targets operational efficiency and consolidation savings -- pricing must reflect that.

## Hybrid Model

| Layer | Model |
|---|---|
| Core platform fee | Institution tier (FTE / enrollment / complexity) |
| Modules | Add-on pricing |
| Infrastructure-heavy services | Usage-based |
| Implementation | One-time fee |
| Support tiers | Optional add-on |

## Institution Tiers

Based on:
- FTE and enrollment
- Operational complexity
- Multi-campus status

Not "R1 prestige pricing." Target is operationally-focused institutions -- community colleges, workforce institutions -- that are drowning in SaaS sprawl and want consolidation.

## Positioning

The sales conversation is: what are you spending across all of the systems Corveaux replaces? Corveaux should cost less than the stack it displaces, with less operational overhead.
