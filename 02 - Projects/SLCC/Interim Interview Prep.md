# Interim Interview Prep

Mock session 01 (2026-07-11) results, frameworks, and drill list. Panel assumption: AVP chairing, EMT directors, previous director. Enrollment-first language wins the room.

## The OSC stat card (Spring 2026 report of record — NEVER from memory; Ryan cites this document)

**98%** persistence with 3+ coaching connections vs **61%** under three · engaged students **60.5% more likely** to persist term-to-term · **78%** good standing vs **27%** non-engaged · **+0.15** GPA (3+ interactions) · **$78.48**/student/year ($423,789.59 ÷ ~5,400). All on p.3 of the exec summary. **The ~38% (SEM number): cohort students vs ALL students** — and the selection ran AGAINST the finding (cohorts are chosen for elevated risk: 2+ online courses, <15 credits, probation, first-gen, new; the all-student baseline includes established persisters). Three altitudes: hallway = "double-digit improvement"; interview = "coached at-risk students out-retained the college's overall baseline by roughly 38% — and we'd selected the students most likely to leave"; IE room = the registration-timing caveat is MOSTLY CLOSED by design: a census re-run picks up late registrants + emerging churn flags, so cohorts span registration timing (concede only "initial draw skews early; census wave dilutes it"). Fix still available: eligible-but-unassigned comparison. BONUS DESIGN: two entry waves (pre-semester vs census pickup) inside one program = testable evidence on intervention TIMING (does Strong Start beat census-entry, risk-controlled?) — the exec summary's "early and sustained" claim, testable from existing data; census-pickup stratum (fixed moment, fixed criteria) gives another natural comparison seam. **Flag management (the piece that completes it):** OSC works early-alert flags for the ENTIRE population → (1) the all-students baseline is CONTAMINATED with OSC-treated students (flag rescues sit in the control group, dragging it up) — the 38% is conservative TWICE (risk-selected cohort + partially-treated comparison); true effect exceeds every quoted number. (2) Cleanest natural experiment of all: flags are FACULTY-initiated (exogenous selection) and the report's own chart already splits "Resolved by OSC" vs "Resolved by Others" — flagged+OSC-worked vs flagged+otherwise-handled, risk held constant by the flag. (3) Strategic frame: cohorts = planned footprint; flags = the institution routing unplanned risk signal to OSC as the Care Network's response arm — "programs get consolidated; infrastructure gets funded." Summary: doubly-conservative headline, three natural experiments on data pulls, one population-wide function — a research agenda with a budget line, authored by the candidate. Also: +0.3 GPA may be same-vintage as the 38%. Unit collision: OSC $6.54 = per student per MONTH; Bruin Connect $6.54 = per INTERACTION.

## The one-sentence thesis

The center has stranded capacity, the institution has friction, I wrote the plan that converts one into the fix for the other, and the interim period is when it starts.

## The two weak muscles

1. **Mechanism, not philosophy.** Every 3-scored answer described values; the fix is always one concrete process sentence (first-week one-on-ones; service accounts + SOPs + IE pipeline; demand-model-driven budget requests).
2. **Land the last sentence.** Repeated pattern: strong openings, leaking endings (hedges, concessions, trailing qualifiers). Rule: end on evidence or commitment, never apology. Say the sentence, stop talking.

## Set pieces (rehearse verbatim)

- **Opener close:** "This wouldn't be a holding pattern. It would be a seamless continuation of work you've already seen — because I'm the one who started it."
- **Three interim commitments:** (1) stability: service levels, staffing, systems steady through the search; (2) protect the reporting migration to institutional platforms — I built that layer; (3) momentum on the caseload redesign and the capacity work leadership has already seen.
- **Why now:** "This center is mid-transition — reporting moving institutional, the outreach redesign mid-flight, capacity work in front of leadership. An interim who learns those from a binder loses the year. I'm the continuity for all three."
- **Fellow applicants:** "It isn't a no, it's a not this time" + the mechanism: "First week, I sit down with each one-on-one, name it directly, and align on what they own and where they want to grow. Ambiguity breeds resentment, not the outcome."
- **Who does your job:** Three answers. (1) By design nothing runs on me personally — service accounts, SOPs, versioned definitions. (2) The reporting model is in IE's publication pipeline; refresh and maintenance become automated and institutionally hosted. (3) The judgment layer comes with me into the seat — one seat instead of two problems. Honest beat: development velocity slows; operations won't feel it. NEVER imply the role is redundant: "doesn't depend on me" is resilience, "isn't needed" is redundancy — I built the first. The role levels up as the center grows into Bruin Connect; pattern is stretch up, backfill transactional with seasonal.
- **People stories:** (1) Bereavement/attendance counseling — check in, topic, counseling, next steps; add result: documentation in, absences excused, issue never recurred. (2) Loss prevention de-escalation — combative individual, connected person-first, trespass notice + comped food + housing/job/food-security referrals; the Bruin Connect ethos as an anecdote. Add result beat. Say "individual," not "subject." End present-tense: "I supervise our outbound team today and I'm coaching the floor through new call-documentation standards this month."
- **THE PEOPLE PILLAR (upgraded 7/13 — lead with this, stories become evidence under it):** "People operations aren't new to me — I owned them end to end for a 120-person team at Nike: hiring, onboarding, training plans, corrective action from first warning through termination. This department is a tenth of that scale. What's new is SLCC's specific processes — and I've spent eighteen months inside them, including rewriting our policy manual against them." For supervising-fellow-applicants, add the spine: "I've managed the people side professionally — I know how to have that conversation, document it correctly, and make sure the relationship survives it. I've been the HR manager in the room, not just read about it." Target investigations available if probed on hardest-conversation experience.
- **Budget:** Present tense first: vendor management, procurement, labor — the biggest line items; procurement/AP relationships. Schedules = expensive resources on high-impact work, less expensive at the hinges. Nike: budget center for a 120-employee operation. Add: "Build the request from the demand model, track actuals against plan, defend every ask with data — my proposals already arrive costed." Capstone: "We just entered the new fiscal year, and I was part of shaping this budget as we planned for it. I wouldn't be learning this year's budget — I'd be executing one that already has my fingerprints on it." New (7/13): "I hold my own tools to the same test I hold vendors to — I've already cut a line from the budget I'm asking to run" (TDX per-tech licensing, cut pre-billing).
- **Doing → leading (the automation answer):** If asked how he'll stop doing and start leading: "I've spent months automating my current role so the department doesn't lose the analyst when it gains a director. Delegation to machines first — so the humans get the judgment work." The Backbone in one sentence. NEVER say "I don't know what director work looks like" — the true version is: "The parts of the seat I haven't held are the parts nobody holds before sitting in it — and roughly forty percent of it, vendor governance, scheduling architecture, policy authorship, reporting, I hold today." Private self-watch (not for the room): in the seat, build-work will tempt as refuge from people-work; the tell is polishing a dashboard the week a 1:1 slips twice.
- **Degree question:** Facts fast (upgraded 7/13): "I'm in graduate coursework today, inside WGU's integrated bachelor's-to-master's program. BSIT confers October, MSIT February." (Count-free phrasing on purpose: shared grad courses are E015/E012/E011 per his tracker, reducing MSIT 11 courses → 8; E015 is open now. Never quote a count that the registrar's view might not match.) Lead with the active graduate standing, not the pending bachelor's — it reframes the applicant from "almost has a BS" to "already a graduate student with conferral dates."
- **Credential mechanics (confirmed 7/14, mentor):** courses complete late Oct → conferral ~2 weeks (~mid-Nov) → MSIT schedule added manually (~2 weeks; November = processing month) → **MSIT term starts Dec 1** → Feb completion at demonstrated pace (21 CUs cleared in June alone; term is add-as-you-clear, 42 CUs remain). Precision phrasing: "complete in October, confers mid-November." Anyone verifying Nov+ sees a conferred bachelor's. **MSIT start is HARD-GATED at 12/1 — cannot begin earlier**, so finishing the BSIT early buys nothing; the optimal pace is steady (~3.5 CUs/week: 56 CUs open across 16 working weeks to end of October), no sprints required or rewarded. Shared grad set confirmed via his tracker: E015 Project Management (open now), E012 IT Mgmt & Leadership, E011 Technical Communication — MSIT drops 11 → 8 courses. Resume line updated 7/14 to count-free "graduate-level coursework currently in progress." "By the end of fall semester I'll hold the degree this posting asks for; by spring, one it doesn't." Dual associates line: "Business administration and psychology — which, honestly, is this job: operations on one side, people on the other." Frame: credential vs learning — "The credential is finishing on a clock. The learning never stopped; my classroom was supervising 120 people and building the systems this center runs on." Never say "lacking." Ender: "the degree formalizes experience I've already accrued."
- **Tiered model (capacity question):** Tier 0 Brutus deflects (88-89% match, 24/7). Tier 1 center triages/resolves (~103 sec, ~$6.54/interaction). Tier 2 specialists see only warm escalations. Volume is exogenous; utilization is set by routing architecture, not team effort. Never let "9.6%" arrive before the frame.
