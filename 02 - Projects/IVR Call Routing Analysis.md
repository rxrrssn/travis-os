# IVR Call Routing Analysis

## What It Is

An active analysis of SLCC's IVR and call routing architecture, framing the operational and student experience cost of the current transfer-heavy phone model.

Deliverable: executive-friendly one-pager. Not a raw telecom report -- strategy consulting / institutional transformation style.

## Core Metrics

- ~83,907 calls analyzed
- ~3.2 average transfer depth
- ~27 seconds average IVR traversal time per menu node
- ~45% of total call duration consumed by overhead (IVR + hold + routing) before reaching help
- ~2,400-2,500 cumulative student hours lost annually navigating systems before receiving assistance

The 2,400-2,500 student hours figure is the emotional core of the deliverable.

## Deliverable Description

One-pager format. Executive-ready. Designed for directors, AVP layer, SEM/EMT.

Components:
- Strong headline metrics
- Visual transfer-flow concept (Sankey diagram or similar flow visualization)
- Estimated cumulative student impact (the ~2,400-2,500 hours figure)
- Operational recommendations
- Current state vs future state framing

Style: strategy consulting / institutional transformation. NOT telecom reporting.

## Audience

- Directors (Contact Center, Enrollment, Student Affairs)
- AVP layer
- Enrollment leadership
- Student Affairs leadership
- Potentially SEM (Strategic Enrollment Management) / EMT (Executive Management Team)

## Stakeholder Scope

Departments touched by the routing analysis:
- Enrollment Services
- Advising

- Registrar
- Bursar
- Academic departments
- Student Affairs
- Marketing / Web

## Philosophical Underpinning

> "Students should not need to organizationally understand the college in order to get help."

The current architecture requires students to know who owns their problem before they can reach the right person. The IVR structure mirrors internal org structure, not student mental models.

The emotional argument: students are spending literal months of cumulative human time trying to find the right person.

## What This Analysis Feeds Into

This is not just a phone report -- it is an input into a broader institutional direction:

- Centralized intake model (one-front-door)
- Reduced transfer dependency
- Simplified student access pathways
- Rationalized IVR architecture
- Potential reduction of publicly advertised direct department lines
- Increased first-contact resolution rates
- Teams Phone strategy discussions
- Long-term operational redesign of student communication access

Connects directly to the [[Bruin Connect Transition]] direction.
