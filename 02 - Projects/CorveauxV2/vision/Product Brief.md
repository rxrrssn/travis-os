# Product Brief

## What Corveaux Is

Corveaux is an Institutional Operating System.

It models institutions directly and renders that model as role-aware experiences across every surface.

It is not a CMS. Not an AI product. Not a catalog replacement. Not a consulting service. Not a demo generator.

It is the platform on which institutions eventually operate.

---

## What Corveaux Builds

An institutional model from everything an institution publishes about itself, structured into canonical primitives and rendered as content blocks across every audience and surface.

**Surfaces Corveaux renders:**
- Website
- Academic catalog
- Internal knowledge base
- Staff and faculty portal
- Student-facing portal
- Search
- Institutional assistant
- Governance and analytics layer

---

## First Product

The first product is the Institutional Knowledge Layer.

Not because it is the final vision. Because it is the easiest manifestation of the vision.

**Components:**
1. Institutional Discovery — Website + Catalog + Directory → Institutional Model
2. Content Block Generation — Institutional knowledge becomes reusable blocks
3. Role-Aware Rendering — One institutional reality, rendered differently for each audience
4. Governance Layer — Ownership, approval, auditability, review cycles
5. Generated Tenant — The mirror and the first production environment

---

## The Generated Tenant

The generated tenant is:
1. The mirror — shows the institution itself, organized
2. The institution's first production environment

It is not disposable. It is the beginning of platform adoption.

---

## Role-Aware Rendering

The same institutional knowledge, rendered for each audience:

| Audience | What they see |
|---|---|
| Visitor | What does this institution do? How do I connect? |
| Prospective Student | Programs, application process, costs, outcomes |
| Current Student | My requirements, services, deadlines, advisor |
| Faculty | Courses, committees, internal processes |
| Staff | Contacts, service boundaries, SOPs |
| Administrator | Content ownership, freshness, gaps, governance |
