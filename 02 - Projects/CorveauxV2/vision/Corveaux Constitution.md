# Corveaux Constitution

These principles are expected to remain stable regardless of product evolution.

---

## One Reality. Many Projections.

Reality is canonical.

Experiences are projections.

---

## Time Is Native

Historical truth is never destroyed.

State changes through events.

Not deletion.

---

## People Change. Roles Persist.

Institutions outlive individuals.

Authority transfers through roles.

Not people.

---

## Authority Emerges

Authority is derived from relationships, policies, and time.

Permissions are projections.

Authority is canonical.

---

## Canonical Before Projection

No projection may become a system of record.

All projections derive from canonical reality.

---

## No Duplicate Reality

A concept should exist once.

Everything else references it.

---

## Institutional Memory Is Sacred

Knowledge should survive turnover.

Continuity should survive personnel changes.

Institutions should not forget.

---

## Final Test

Every decision must answer:

> Does this make it easier or harder for Corveaux to model and operate the institution?

If harder, reconsider.
