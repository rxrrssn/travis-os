# Institutional Maturity Model

## Level 1 — Fragmented

Information exists.

Ownership is unclear.

Knowledge is distributed across systems.

The institution relies heavily on institutional memory.

Most organizations begin here.

---

## Level 2 — Documented

Information becomes centralized.

Policies become discoverable.

Basic governance begins emerging.

The institution starts documenting itself.

---

## Level 3 — Structured

Knowledge becomes organized.

Ownership becomes explicit.

Relationships between institutional concepts become visible.

The institution begins understanding itself.

---

## Level 4 — Governed

Policies, authority, and stewardship become formally managed.

Knowledge remains current.

Ownership becomes durable.

The institution develops continuity.

---

## Level 5 — Integrated

Systems begin operating from shared institutional reality.

Workflows become institution-aware.

Communication becomes context-aware.

The institution develops coherence.

---

## Level 6 — Institutional Operating System

The institution operates directly from its canonical model.

Reality is managed once.

Experiences become projections.

Institutional continuity becomes native.

---

## Objective

The goal of Corveaux is not software adoption.

The goal is institutional maturity.
