# Why Institutions Fail To Understand Themselves

Institutions do not become fragmented overnight.

Fragmentation accumulates.

A new department is created.

A new system is purchased.

A new process is introduced.

A new exception is granted.

Each decision solves a local problem.

Few decisions consider the institution as a whole.

---

## The Visibility Problem

Most people see only the portion of the institution they interact with.

Departments understand departments.

Employees understand roles.

Systems understand domains.

Few things understand the institution.

---

## The Coordination Problem

Information exists.

Ownership exists.

Authority exists.

Knowledge exists.

The challenge is that these things rarely exist in the same place.

The institution spends increasing amounts of effort reconciling itself.

---

## The Time Problem

People leave.

Knowledge leaves.

Context disappears.

Institutions repeatedly solve problems they have solved before.

The cycle continues.

---

## The Corveaux Hypothesis

Institutions struggle because they lack a coherent understanding of themselves.

If institutional reality can be modeled directly, governance improves.

Continuity improves.

Knowledge persists.

Communication improves.

The institution becomes capable of understanding itself again.

That is the hypothesis Corveaux exists to validate.
