# Philosophy

## One Reality. Many Projections.

The institution is canonical. Everything else is a projection.

The website is not the system of record.
The catalog is not the system of record.
The assistant is not the system of record.
The portal is not the system of record.

They are all rendered surfaces.

The canonical layer is institutional knowledge.

---

## Institutional Memory

Institutions forget.
People leave.
Knowledge becomes fragmented.
Policies become disconnected from implementation.

Corveaux preserves institutional memory.

The institution remembers. People should not have to.

---

## Time Is Native

Everything is temporal.

Every entity, relationship, event, policy, and authority exists within time.

Corveaux simultaneously models historical state, current state, and future state.

An enrollment record that is reversed is not deleted. A reversal event is added. The record of what happened is permanent.

---

## Authority Emerges

Authority is not assigned.

Authority emerges from Relationships + Policies + Time.

Permissions are projections.
Authority is canonical.

---

## Reintroducing Institutions to Themselves

Institutions have published enormous amounts of information about themselves. That information lives in silos. The website team doesn't know what the catalog says. The advisor doesn't know what the website says. The prospective student can't reconcile any of it.

Corveaux takes everything an institution publishes about itself, structures it into a canonical knowledge layer, and renders it as audience-aware experiences.

The generated tenant is the mirror. The scrape is the mechanism. The governance layer provides continuity.

---

## The Final Principle

Every architectural decision must pass a single test:

> Does this make it easier or harder for Corveaux to eventually model and operate the entire institution?

If it makes that harder, it is the wrong decision.
If it makes that easier, it is probably the right decision.
