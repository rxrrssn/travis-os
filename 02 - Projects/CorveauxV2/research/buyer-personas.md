# Buyer Personas  
  
**Status:** Draft. Research pending.  
  
## Purpose  
  
Identify who experiences the pain Corveaux addresses, who champions change internally, who controls budget, and who ultimately approves adoption.  
  
The initial go-to-market buyer may not be the long-term platform buyer.  
  
This document captures hypotheses to validate through institutional research and customer conversations.  
  
---  
  
## Persona Types  
  
### Economic Buyer  
  
Controls budget, procurement, and contract approval.  
  
May not experience the operational pain directly.  
  
### Champion  
  
Believes the problem exists, advocates internally, builds consensus, and helps navigate institutional politics.  
  
May or may not control budget.  
  
### User  
  
Experiences the pain directly and provides requirements, feedback, and validation.  
  
### Approver  
  
Possesses formal authority to authorize procurement, strategic initiatives, or institutional change.  
  
At some institutions this is the same person as the economic buyer. At others it is not.  
  
---  
  
## Primary Buyer Hypothesis  
  
### CIO / VP of Strategic Initiatives  
  
Hypothesis:  
  
The initial Corveaux buyer is likely a leader whose authority crosses organizational boundaries and who can justify solving problems that span:  
  
- Website governance  
- Catalog management  
- Knowledge management  
- Student experience  
- Enrollment journeys  
- Institutional operations  
  
Corveaux's initial value proposition crosses silos. The buyer must therefore have cross-silo authority.  
  
### Pain Signals  
  
- Multiple disconnected institutional systems  
- Repeated content duplication  
- Inconsistent information across surfaces  
- Website governance problems  
- Catalog maintenance burden  
- Knowledge ownership ambiguity  
- AI initiatives without institutional structure  
- Difficulty understanding institutional relationships and responsibilities  
  
### Success Metrics  
  
- Reduced content duplication  
- Improved governance visibility  
- Faster information updates  
- Improved institutional consistency  
- Reduced operational friction

## Secondary Buyer Hypotheses  
  
### Web Director  
  
Primary Pain:  
  
Content governance.  
  
Symptoms:  
  
- Content exists in many places  
- Ownership is unclear  
- Pages become stale  
- Departments publish conflicting information  
- Large website management overhead  
  
### VP Academic Affairs  
  
Primary Pain:  
  
Catalog governance.  
  
Symptoms:  
  
- Curriculum information is difficult to maintain  
- Program information exists in multiple systems  
- Approval workflows are complex  
- Institutional knowledge is fragmented  
  
### VP Enrollment  
  
Primary Pain:  
  
Prospective student experience.  
  
Symptoms:  
  
- Program information is difficult to navigate  
- Students struggle to find accurate information  
- Website journeys are inconsistent  
- Enrollment communications require extensive manual effort  
  
### Director of Institutional Effectiveness  
  
Primary Pain:  
  
Institutional visibility and governance.  
  
Symptoms:  
  
- Difficult to identify ownership  
- Difficult to measure information quality  
- Institutional knowledge is fragmented across systems

These buyers may become relevant after the initial entry wedge.  
  
### COO  
  
Interest:  
  
Operational efficiency and institutional coordination.  
  
### Provost  
  
Interest:  
  
Academic operations, governance, and institutional effectiveness.  
  
### President  
  
Interest:  
  
Institution-wide visibility, strategy, and transformation.  
  
### System Office Leadership  
  
Interest:  
  
Multi-institution governance, standardization, and oversight.

## Institutional Purchasing Questions  
  
### Procurement

- [ ] Which approvals require legal review?

- [ ] Who can approve implementation without Faculty Senate involvement?  
- [ ] What initiatives require cabinet-level approval?  
- [ ] How are cross-functional initiatives governed?  
  
### Budget  
  
- [ ] What discretionary technology budgets exist?  
- [ ] Who controls them?  
- [ ] How are innovation projects funded?

- [ ] Which vendors are difficult to displace?  
- [ ] Which contracts are already in place?  
  
### Strategic Discovery  
  
- [ ] What problem is painful enough that a CIO will spend political capital to solve it?  
- [ ] What problem is painful enough that a cabinet-level leader will sponsor it?  
- [ ] Which problems create urgency versus interest?  
  
### Platform Discovery  
  
- [ ] Does the buyer see Corveaux as software, infrastructure, or institutional capability?  
- [ ] When does the conversation shift from knowledge management to institutional operations?  
- [ ] Which executive becomes the buyer once Corveaux expands beyond the entry wedge?

## Working Hypothesis  
  
The initial buyer is likely a CIO or equivalent cross-functional technology leader.  
  
The initial champion is likely closer to the operational pain:  
  
- Web Director  
- Institutional Effectiveness  
- Strategic Initiatives  
- Enrollment Operations  
  
The long-term platform buyer may ultimately become:  
  
- COO  
- Provost  
- President  
- System Office Leadership  
  
The initial sale and the eventual Institutional Operating System sale may involve different stakeholders.
