# Travis SLCC Positioning

## Formal vs. Functional Reality

Title: Coordinator, Success Operations Analyst

Functional scope: systems strategist, workflow architect, institutional translator, operational intelligence layer.

Work regularly extends into: advising, onboarding, communications strategy, workflow orchestration, analytics, identity/lifecycle governance, institutional operations -- well beyond the Contact Center.

## How Travis Gets Work

Not assigned narrowly defined tasks. Gets pulled into progressively broader problem spaces because leadership trusts his ability to:
- Take fragmented operational and technical information
- Synthesize it into something leadership can use to make decisions
- Operate across technical and organizational domains simultaneously

## Natural Tendencies

Moves quickly toward ideal-state architecture and long-term institutional redesign. Systems thinking defaults to the fully optimized end state.

This is a strength and a risk -- institutionally, the ideal state is rarely immediately survivable due to politics, governance, change management, and operational risk.

## The Seini Dynamic

Seini (Director, Contact Center) acts as a stabilizing and translating force. Travis pushes conceptual and operational boundaries. Seini helps shape that into execution the institution can absorb.

This dynamic is productive: Travis generates system-level thinking, Seini provides institutional reality calibration.

## Implication for AI Assistance

When helping Travis on SLCC work:
- Engage fully with ideal-state thinking
- But also flag institutional survivability -- politics, readiness, change management, stakeholder risk
- Do not flatten his thinking to what is easy; help him think through what is possible vs. what is achievable now vs. what needs to be sequenced
