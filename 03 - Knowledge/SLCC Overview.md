# SLCC Overview

## Institution

Salt Lake Community College - large multi-campus community college in Utah.

Student populations: traditional, adult learners, workforce education, technical programs, transfer pathways, online.

## Operational Character

- Highly decentralized
- Heavily siloed
- Vendor fragmented
- Process layered
- Operationally inconsistent between departments

Core institutional problem: systems evolved independently instead of intentionally.

Consequences:
- Duplicate communication
- Disconnected student experiences
- Inconsistent operational ownership
- Fragmented reporting
- Excessive manual work
- Student navigation friction

## Recurring Institutional Themes

- Siloed ownership
- Heavy middleware reliance
- Inconsistent communication
- Duplicate systems
- Process inefficiency

## Strategic Principle

The institution should feel like ONE organization to the student.

## HB 265 Context

Utah HB 265 Phase 1 resulted in academic affairs budget cuts and program closures. Post-restructure, leadership has heightened focus on measurable outcomes from student-facing teams. Teams that can demonstrate metrics are protected and expanded. See [[HB 265]].

## Student Affairs Departments Roster

SLCC Student Affairs isn't a clean department list -- it's organized into functional clusters, and units cross-list between them. Reference roster by cluster:

| Cluster | Units |
|---|---|
| Dean of Students | Dean of Students Office, Accessibility & Disability Services, Center for Health & Counseling, Behavioral Intervention Team (BIT) |
| Enrollment Management | Admissions, Concurrent Enrollment, Contact Center, Financial Aid & Scholarships, Office of the Registrar & Academic Records |
| Student Services (ops/support) | Child Care & Family Services, International Student Services, Student Center (operations & facilities), Student Services North, Student Services South, Veterans Services |
| Student Success (retention/coaching/engagement) | Academic Advising, Career Services, Orientation & Student Success, PACE (Partnership for Accessing College Education), Pre-College Engagement & Transition, Student Engagement Experience & Achievement, Thayne Center (Student Life, Leadership & Community Engagement), TRIO Programs |
| Health, Wellness & Recreation | Center for Health & Counseling (medical + mental health), Lifetime Activities Center (recreation), Dental Clinic, OTA/PTA Clinics |
| Extended/Often-Overlooked | Bruin Pantry, Printing Services, Peer Coaching, Dream Center, Community Writing Center, Engaged Learning (Study Abroad & Civic Engagement), ePortfolio Support |

Explicitly NOT Student Affairs, despite student-facing overlap: Academic Schools (Business, Health Sciences, etc.), IT/Facilities/Finance, faculty departments.

Operational read: Contact Center + Advising + Registrar + Financial Aid is the operational backbone cluster; everything else is a support layer feeding student success and retention.
