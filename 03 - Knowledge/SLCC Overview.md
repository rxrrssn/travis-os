# SLCC Overview

## Institution

Salt Lake Community College - large multi-campus community college in Utah.

Student populations: traditional, adult learners, workforce education, technical programs, transfer pathways, online.

## Operational Character

- Highly decentralized
- Heavily siloed
- Vendor fragmented
- Process layered
- Operationally inconsistent between departments

Core institutional problem: systems evolved independently instead of intentionally.

Consequences:
- Duplicate communication
- Disconnected student experiences
- Inconsistent operational ownership
- Fragmented reporting
- Excessive manual work
- Student navigation friction

## Recurring Institutional Themes

- Siloed ownership
- Heavy middleware reliance
- Inconsistent communication
- Duplicate systems
- Process inefficiency

## Strategic Principle

The institution should feel like ONE organization to the student.

## HB 265 Context

Utah HB 265 Phase 1 resulted in academic affairs budget cuts and program closures. Post-restructure, leadership has heightened focus on measurable outcomes from student-facing teams. Teams that can demonstrate metrics are protected and expanded. See [[HB 265]].
