# Corveaux Regulatory Surface

## Overview

Regulatory exposure expands significantly as modules touch student records, financial aid, HR/payroll, advising, and accessibility. Must be addressed proactively -- not retrofitted.

---

## FERPA

Required at any module touching student data.

Needs:
- RBAC with legitimate educational interest enforcement
- Audit logs for all access and disclosures
- Granular access controls
- Disclosure tracking

---

## Accessibility (WCAG)

Critical in higher ed -- matters even at the CMS/Web stage.

Needs:
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Keyboard navigation throughout
- Screen reader support
- Accessible authoring tools for content editors

---

## Title IV / Financial Aid

Extremely high regulatory risk. Deep FA processing should be avoided initially.

Strategy: integrate first, replace later -- if ever.

---

## State Records Retention Laws

Vary by state. Must support:
- Configurable retention policies
- Immutable audit trails
- Export and archive capabilities

---

## AI Governance

As AI features expand into advising and student-risk contexts:

- Explainability requirements
- Human override capabilities
- Disclosure to students
- Prompt governance and institutional controls
- Bias and equity auditing

Especially sensitive in: advising notes, risk scoring, intervention recommendations.
