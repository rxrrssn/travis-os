# Mainstay / Brutus

## What It Is

Mainstay is the institution's chatbot and student messaging platform.

Chatbot identity: **Brutus**

Capabilities: SMS campaigns, automated reminders, student outreach, FAQ automation, lifecycle messaging, engagement nudges.

## Philosophy

**One voice, one Brutus.**

- Unified communication identity
- Centralized messaging strategy
- Lifecycle-aware communication
- Reduced fragmentation

Do NOT want: multiple disconnected bots, inconsistent personalities, siloed campaigns.

## Campaign Segmentation Strategy

- Holds
- Enrollment status
- Engagement risk indicators
- Major / program
- Lifecycle stage
- Support needs
- Operational triggers

## Ideal Future State

Brutus becomes a lifecycle orchestration engine, not just a chatbot.

The student should experience a single institutional voice across the entire lifecycle.

## Metrics (2023 - Spring 2026)

- 218,050 students reached
- 194,419 questions received
- 2.85M messages exchanged
- 98.76% resolved automatically
- 136 engagement campaigns

Top conversation categories: Registrar (27.1%), Admissions (23.5%), Student Services (18.1%), Advising (16.9%).

See [[Brutus Engagement Report Spring 2026]] for full breakdown.

## Gen AI Enhancement (enabled 8/15/25)

Mainstay introduced Generative AI in Knowledge Base and Flash Responses at no additional cost in Fall 2025.

Impact on SLCC bot (FA24 vs FA25):
- Match rate: 74% → 87% (+13 pts)
- Missed questions: 3,631 → 1,426 (-61%)
- Positive feedback: 36% → 44% (+8 pts)

Impact SP25 vs SP26 (to date):
- Match rate: 61% → 89% (+28 pts)
- Missed questions: 5,261 → 49 (-99%)

The bot is now substantively more capable. SP26 missed question count of 49 vs 5,261 prior year represents a near-complete elimination of unanswered questions.

## Bot Usage Patterns

From AY 2025-26 bot data:
- 26% of messages received after hours (5pm-7am)
- 9% of messages received on weekends
- SMS reach is consistently 4-8x web chat reach

## Semesterly Campaign Engagement Trend

Active engagement has risen from ~15% (SP24) to ~20% (SU25-FA25), then dipped to 12.9% in SP26.
Response rates (two-way conversation) have declined over time: 10.7% (SP24) → 5.8% (SP26).
Students increasingly consuming Brutus as a push/notification channel rather than a dialogue channel.

See [[Salt Lake CC Mainstay 2026 Data Report]] for full semester-by-semester breakdown.
